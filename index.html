Projects

Virtual Reality Drunk Driving Simulator

Marshall University

Using Unity3D, I lead the development of an immersive drunk driving simulator to raise awareness of the dangers of driving under the influence. We placed the user in a realistic virtual environment and gave them full control of a virtual vehicle via a Logitech steering wheel and pedal rig. The user experiences 5 increasing levels of blood-alcohol content (BAC) simulated by input manipulation and realtime visual effects. We developed a scoring system to gauge how their driving was affected at each level that takes into account speeding, running stop signs, and lane control.
